<p>Halal stock screening sounds complicated because it's usually explained in academic language. In practice, it's a five-minute check you can run before buying any individual stock. Here's the checklist.</p>

<h2>Step 1: Sector screen</h2>
<p>Rule the stock out immediately if its core business is:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Conventional banking, lending, or insurance</li>
  <li>Alcohol, tobacco, or gambling</li>
  <li>Pork production or processing</li>
  <li>Adult entertainment</li>
  <li>Conventional (interest-based) financial services broadly</li>
</ul>
<p>If the company's core revenue comes from any of these, it fails the sector screen and there's no point running the financial ratios.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Financial ratio screen</h2>
<p>If it clears the sector screen, check three ratios against the company's total assets or market cap, using the most recent balance sheet:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Debt ratio:</strong> interest-bearing debt should generally stay under ~30-33% of total assets or market cap, depending on which screening standard you follow (AAOIFI vs. other common screens).</li>
  <li><strong>Cash and interest-bearing securities:</strong> should generally also stay under a similar ~30-33% threshold.</li>
  <li><strong>Interest income / non-compliant income:</strong> should typically be under ~5% of total revenue.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step 3: Purification, if it passes</h2>
<p>Even compliant companies sometimes carry a small amount of interest income (e.g. from a cash reserve). If so, calculate that percentage of your dividend income and donate the equivalent amount to charity — this "purifies" the return.</p>

<h2>Where to get the numbers fast</h2>
<p>You don't need to build this from scratch every time. Several Islamic screening tools and apps (Zoya, Musaffa, and similar) run this exact checklist automatically and will flag pass/fail with the underlying ratios shown. Manually verifying against the actual balance sheet is still worth doing for anything that's a large position in your portfolio — but for a quick gut check before buying, these tools cover the five-minute version well.</p>

<h2>The bottom line</h2>
<p>Sector screen first, financial ratios second, purification if needed. That's the whole process. It takes longer to explain than to actually do.</p>
